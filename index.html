VIRAT KOHLI

About :

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national cricket team. He was born on November 5, 1988, and is considered one of the best batsmen in the world. In 2018, he became the first player in ICC cricket history to win all three ICC awards in a single year.

Here are some other details about Virat Kohli:

  • He is often called the "king of cricket" by his fans.
  • He made his international debut in 2008 and has represented India in all three formats of the game.
  • He comes from a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Kohli, was a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, was a housewife.
  • He has an older brother, Vikas, and an older sister, Bhawna.

Career in Cricket :

As of November 2023, Kohli has 80 centuries in international cricket, including 29 in Test cricket, 50 in One Day Internationals (ODIs), and one in Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is). He is also the fastest batsman to reach 10,000 and 11,000 runs in ODI cricket, achieving the milestones in 205 and 222 innings respectively.

Here are some of Kohli's other achievements:

  • T20 Internationals:
      • Kohli leads all batsmen in most career runs with 4,008 runs. He also has the highest batting average by any player in T20 Internationals at 52.73.
  • ODIs:
      • Kohli was India's top run-scorer in ODI tournaments for three consecutive years – 2010, 2011, and 2012. He also won the ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year Award in 2012.
  • U19 World Cup :
      • In early 2008, Kohli led India to victory in the U19 World Cup in Kuala Lumpur.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, Virat Kohli’s remarkable journey is a blend of talent, hard work, and an unwavering commitment to excellence. His legacy is not just about cricketing records, but also about transforming the sport and inspiring millions.
